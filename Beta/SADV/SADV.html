Violence against women is happening right now. In every country and corner of the world, there is a woman who is being abused and battered. According to the World Health Organization, one in three women have been abused physically or sexually in her lifetime, and one in five have been abused as children.

It’s easy to get confused by all of the different terms used in the movement to end violence against women. Most likely you’ve also heard of sexual assault or domestic violence. You may have even heard of intimate partner violence or gender based violence. Each of these terms has a slightly different definition. We will be talking about all of these different issues under one universal term, violence against women.




Gender based violence is similar term referring to any violence that brings harm to someone because of their gender. It is used more globally, especially in countries where women are considered to be disposable or less valuable than men.
