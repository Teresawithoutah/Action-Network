ACTIVISTS OF THE MONTH


Radio hosts Monica & Veronica


Monica and Veronica are not your average girls. Both of them have worked hard at their studies to overcome poverty and immense obstacles in their education in their home country of Sierra Leone. Right now they are learning how to speak with courage at their radio show, where they educate women in their community about important issues such as teen pregnancy, child marriage, and STDs. Monica and Veronica work hard to understand the topics so that they can answer questions from their listeners. Not all of their listeners have a positive reaction to such difficult topics, but the girls respond with dignity and an original song, declaring “You can believe that we gonna make a better change.” You can believe these girls are fighting for the rights of women in their community.


Podcast host Jess Weiner

Jess Weiner has a pretty cool job: She works with brands to help them connect with women in a more authentic way. But Jess also hosts her own podcast called Talk to Jess, in which she talks to women about how their careers, their ambitions, and what shaped them into who they are today. Through her podcast, she’s able to tackle topics like the representation of women in media and pop culture, and encourages women everywhere to go after their goals with confidence.

1. You always have to think about your audience. What do they need to hear, what do they want to hear, and what will surprise them to hear? If you can think about that, then you’re always going to be one step ahead of your listener. You’re reacting to them, you’re giving to them, but you’re also giving them something they weren’t expecting.

2. Consider your guest. If you’re inviting someone into a conversation, they’re not a prop. They’re not a blank list of generic questions -- they’re a person. It’s very sacred to have a conversation with someone and have them share things about their life with you. So, make sure you’re honoring that by really having the conversation you want to have.

3. Don’t forget to enjoy it. People can hear it in your voice if you’re enjoying a conversation. I know in my podcast, I curse and I laugh a lot. When I listen back, I actually love those moments because it feels like I’m peering into a real conversation versus a staged interview. People can feel that joy and share in it.
